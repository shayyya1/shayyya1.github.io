Passion

I think my first real dive into computational thinking started through playing Minecraft. I had just learned about logic gates in school, and in Minecraft there is redstone which functions the same way. It quickly became a major project of mine to create an addition calculator, completely from first principles, out of logic gates. I started small with a binary half adder, then scaled up to n-bit addition by chaining half adders together, which was easy enough. The real challenge came with integrating denary into the interface. In particular, converting the binary outputs to denary was a tremendously difficult problem. I spent months theorizing and prototyping different solutions, before eventually finding an iterative steady-state based solution. I loved the process of continually developing my knowledge of the problem, and expanding my toolbox through each failed prototype.
